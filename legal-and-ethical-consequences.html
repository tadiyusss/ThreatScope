Liability and Accountability

Laws and Regulations

Liability and Accountability

When companies fail to protect personal data, they can be held legally and financially responsible. Under GDPR, companies can be fined heavily and must demonstrate that they took proper steps to protect data. CCPA allows consumers to sue companies for negligence after a data breach. Executives and IT teams may face internal consequences like termination or demotion. Ignoring security risks is seen as a failure of duty, not just bad management. Laws like GDPR and CCPA are making it clear that accountability for data breaches goes all the way to the top. Legal action can follow if businesses are found to be negligent. Maintaining cybersecurity is a legal requirement, not just a best practice.

Laws and Regulations

  • GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)

    The General Data Protection Regulation is a law from the European Union focused on protecting personal data. Organizations that violate GDPR may face fines up to €20 million or 4% of their annual global turnover. The law applies not only to EU companies but to any business processing EU citizens’ data. GDPR requires companies to obtain clear consent and report breaches within 72 hours. Violations can severely damage both a company’s finances and public trust.

  • CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act)

    The CCPA gives California residents more control over how businesses use their personal information. If a business violates the CCPA, it can be fined $2,500 per violation or $7,500 if intentional. The law mandates transparency about data collection and provides consumers the right to request deletion of their data. Companies must also allow users to opt out of the sale of personal information. Failure to comply can lead to lawsuits and reputational harm.

  • GDPR vs CCPA (Key Differences and Overlap)

    While both GDPR and CCPA focus on personal data protection, GDPR is more strict and applies internationally, whereas CCPA is limited to California. GDPR requires explicit consent for data use, while CCPA emphasizes the right to opt out. Penalties under GDPR are generally more severe than those under CCPA. Both laws encourage businesses to take data protection seriously to avoid legal trouble. Understanding both is essential for global companies handling user data.

Concepts of Cybersecurity / Prominent Ethical Hackers

Prominent Ethical Hackers

Kevin Mitnick

Known to the FBI as the "Most Wanted Hacker," Kevin Mitnick transitioned from a notorious cybercriminal to a leading cybersecurity consultant. He now advises over 500 companies and government agencies worldwide. Mitnick is also an accomplished author, with works like The Art of Deception and The Art of Intrusion.

Tsutomu Shimomura

Tsutomu Shimomura gained fame for assisting the FBI in capturing Kevin Mitnick in 1995. He chronicled this pursuit in his book Takedown. Currently, Shimomura is the CEO and founder of Neofocal, a company specializing in smart LED networks.

Charlie Miller

A former NSA hacker, he is renowned for winning the Pwn2Own contest four times. He was the first to remotely exploit vulnerabilities in iPhones and Android devices. Miller now works at Cruise, focusing on the security of autonomous vehicles

Joanna Rutkowska

Joanna Rutkowska is a cybersecurity expert specializing in rootkit research and development. She founded the Invisible Things Lab, dedicated to enhancing system security. Rutkowska's work has significantly advanced techniques to detect and counter rootkit attacks.

Bruce Scheier

Bruce Schneier is a highly accomplished figure in cybersecurity, with over 30 years of experience. He has made significant contributions to cryptography, security protocols, risk management, and security policy. Schneier's work has benefited renowned companies like Google.

Greg Hoglund

Greg Hoglund is a renowned computer forensics expert known for his work in combating cyber threats. He holds patents for fault injection methods used in software testing. Hoglund founded HBGary, a notable technology security firm that joined the McAfee Security Innovation Alliance in 2008.

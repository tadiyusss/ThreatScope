TruePill Data Breach

John Louie Cuenco

April 11, 2025

A significant class-action lawsuit brought serious allegations against Oracle, a prominent data broker, accusing the company of engaging in clandestine surveillance of individuals' online and offline activities. The lawsuit contended that Oracle compiled intricate profiles on individuals, encompassing sensitive details such as their location data, financial status, and health information. Furthermore, it was alleged that this wealth of personal data was then sold to third-party entities without the knowledge or explicit consent of the individuals being tracked. A key aspect of the lawsuit highlighted Oracle's purported use of "coretag" tracking technology across numerous websites. This tracking allegedly enabled Oracle to intercept communications and gather data from individuals with whom the company had no direct relationship or interaction.

In a preliminary move towards resolving the legal dispute, Oracle reached a settlement agreement that entails a payment of $115 million to consumers who were affected by these data collection practices. Additionally, the settlement mandates that Oracle implement certain changes to its business operations, presumably to enhance transparency and address the privacy concerns raised in the lawsuit. The final judicial approval for this settlement is currently scheduled for November 14, 2024, and further comprehensive details regarding the settlement terms and eligibility for compensation are available on a dedicated settlement website. This legal action and the resulting settlement underscore the increasing public and regulatory scrutiny surrounding the often-hidden data collection practices employed by data brokers and the significant implications these practices have for individual consumer privacy rights.

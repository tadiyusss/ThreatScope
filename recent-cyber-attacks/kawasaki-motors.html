Kawasaki Motors Europe Cyber Attack

Jess Patrick Paguel

April 05, 2025

I n September 2024, Kawasaki Motors Europe (KME) became a target of the RansomHub ransomware group, who claimed to have exfiltrated a substantial 487 gigabytes of data from their systems. Initially, Kawasaki downplayed the incident, stating that the cyberattack was not successful. However, despite their efforts to isolate servers and implement a data cleansing process, RansomHub contradicted this claim by leaking the allegedly stolen information on their dark web portal. The compromised data reportedly included sensitive business documents, financial records, banking information, dealership details, and internal communications, exposing potentially confidential information.

The risk posed by this attack primarily affected Kawasaki's business operations and its stakeholders. The leaked data could potentially be exploited for various malicious purposes, including financial fraud, competitive intelligence gathering, or further cyberattacks targeting Kawasaki's partners and customers. While Kawasaki reported that over 90% of their server functionality was restored relatively quickly, allowing business operations to resume, the fact that the sensitive data was leaked indicates a failure to meet the ransom demands of the RansomHub group. The solution in this case involved Kawasaki refusing to pay the ransom, leading to the public release of the stolen data and forcing the company to deal with the potential fallout from the data breach.

Consejo Nacional Electoral

Overview How it Happened Data Compromised

Overview of the Breach

In early 2025, the Venezuelan National Electoral Council (CNE) experienced a sophisticated cyberattack aimed at disrupting the country’s electoral processes. The attack targeted the CNE's digital infrastructure, which includes voter registration databases and other election-related systems. This breach, though not fully attributed, is suspected to have been carried out by politically motivated actors or advanced persistent threats (APTs) with national or international motives. It occurred during a critical time as the country was preparing for national elections, raising concerns about the integrity and security of the electoral process. The cyberattack caused delays in election-related services and undermined public confidence in the system. As election-related data was targeted, the attack also heightened fears about the potential for election manipulation or interference. The incident brought to light the vulnerability of democratic institutions to cyber threats, stressing the need for stronger digital security in electoral systems worldwide.

How the Breach Happened

  • Exploitation of Vulnerabilities in Public-Facing Systems

    The attackers are believed to have exploited vulnerabilities in the CNE’s public-facing systems, which are used for voter registration and electoral processes. These exposed services likely served as an entry point, allowing the attackers initial access to the network. This method of exploitation is commonly associated with advanced persistent threat (APT) groups, who often target low-profile vulnerabilities in publicly accessible portals.

  • Sustained Attack Campaign

    Once inside, the attackers likely aimed to access sensitive data such as voter registration information and election records. It is suspected that the cyberattack was carried out by an APT group that employed advanced tactics to remain undetected and escalate their access over time. APT groups are known for their sophisticated, sustained attack campaigns aimed at high-value institutions like electoral bodies to cause disruptions and create instability.

  • Malware Origin

    The malware used in the attack was linked to previous attacks on Russian banks, suggesting that the attackers were using familiar tools, potentially linked to state-sponsored or organized cybercriminal groups.

Data Compromised

  • Voter Registration Database

    The primary target of the attack was the CNE's voter registration database, which contained sensitive personal information about millions of Venezuelan citizens. If the data was accessed, it could have been used for political manipulation, identity theft, or other malicious activities. Given the nature of the breach, there were concerns that the attackers might have altered or destroyed critical voter data to disrupt the elections. This posed a major threat to the democratic process in Venezuela, where the integrity of the election is crucial for public trust.

  • Election-Related Systems

    Beyond voter data, the attack also targeted election-related applications used for polling, result tabulation, and communication between electoral officials. These systems, if compromised, could have led to the manipulation of election outcomes or a breakdown in communication between local and national electoral bodies. The breach raised alarms about the security and redundancy of election infrastructure in Venezuela and other nations with similarly vulnerable systems.

Impact on the Company and Customers

The cyberattack on the Venezuelan National Electoral Council (CNE) created significant disruptions in the country’s electoral process, resulting in delays to critical systems and the temporary suspension of certain electoral services. Public confidence in the integrity of the election system was severely damaged, as citizens and international observers questioned the ability of the CNE to securely manage voter data and election results. Political tensions escalated as accusations of foreign interference or domestic sabotage circulated, further undermining the credibility of the electoral process. The breach also prompted a swift response from the Venezuelan government, which launched investigations into the incident and began reassessing its cybersecurity measures for national elections. In the aftermath, there were calls for stronger safeguards, including multi-factor authentication and encrypted communication channels, to prevent similar attacks in the future. The breach also sparked international debates on the security of digital voting systems and the vulnerability of democratic institutions to cyberattacks. As a result, Venezuela’s electoral infrastructure is expected to undergo a significant overhaul, with increased investment in cybersecurity and better coordination with international organizations to bolster election security.

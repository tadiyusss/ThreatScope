Merck

Overview How it Happened Data Compromised

Overview of the Breach

The NotPetya hack had a major effect on pharmaceutical behemoth Merck & Co. in June 2017. Initially targeted at Ukrainian systems, the malware quickly expanded throughout the world, impacting Merck among other multinational corporations. Merck, one of the biggest pharmaceutical companies, experienced significant disruptions in its business, research, and production processes. Over 30,000 computers were rendered inoperable by the hack, which also caused a delay in the production of vital medications and vaccines. Using the EternalBlue vulnerability, the attack took advantage of flaws in out-of-date Windows systems. Merck promptly started recovery measures after officially confirming the occurrence. The onslaught, which is regarded as one of the most damaging malware incidents in history, was ascribed to state-sponsored Russian actors.

How the Breach Happened

Initial Infection via M.E.Doc and Exploitation of EternalBlue

Through a corrupted upgrade to a Ukrainian accounting program named M.E.Doc, the NotPetya malware gained access to systems. Merck did not deploy the program, but once within a global network, the malware's worm-like characteristics allowed it to spread quickly. The EternalBlue exploit, a flaw in Microsoft's SMB protocol that was made public by the hacker collective Shadow Brokers, was used in the attack. Systems were left unprotected and susceptible to infection, even if they had not yet installed the available Windows fixes.

Destructive Payload and Impact on Merck's Operations

NotPetya was a "wiper" that permanently corrupted data, yet it functioned similarly to ransomware. Without complete backups, recovery would be impossible because it encrypted systems and erased important boot information. The quick spread was facilitated by Merck's network segmentation and antiquated security protocols. Systems utilized for email correspondence, logistics, and potentially medicine manufacture were rendered inoperable by the infection.

Data Compromised

Operational Disruption and System Recovery

The attack resulted in a significant loss of operational data but did not target financial or personal data. Important documents and systems pertaining to internal logistics and medication production were made unavailable. Shipments of medications were delayed and vaccine production was temporarily stopped down as a result of the damage. Over the course of several months, Merck was forced to rebuild the impacted systems using backups.

Communication Breakdowns and Supply Chain Issues

Email, scheduling, and documentation tools were among the communication platforms that experienced disruptions. In the immediate aftermath, this led to lost production and internal disarray. Delays and difficulties with fulfillment resulted from the impact on supply chain systems as well. For a while, Merck was unable to provide important drugs like Gardasil and other vaccines.

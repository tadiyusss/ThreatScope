Marriott International

Overview How it Happened Data Compromised

Overview of the Breach

In November 2018, Marriott International revealed that attackers had compromised the Starwood guest reservation database, exposing the personal information of up to 500 million guests. The breach began as early as 2014 but was not detected until late 2018. The compromised data included sensitive details such as names, mailing addresses, phone numbers, email addresses, passport numbers, and payment card information. The breach was attributed to a suspected Chinese state-sponsored hacking group, underscoring the risks of insufficient cybersecurity due diligence during mergers and acquisitions. This attack raised concerns about the vulnerabilities of large multinational corporations and their ability to secure legacy systems inherited through acquisitions.

How the Breach Happened

Long-Term Infiltration of Starwood Systems

The attackers initially infiltrated the Starwood network years before Marriott's acquisition of Starwood in 2016. The compromise went undetected for years, allowing the attackers to establish a persistent foothold within the system and gain continuous access to sensitive data.

Advanced Malware and Data Exfiltration

The attackers used advanced malware to extract large volumes of guest data from the compromised Starwood systems over several years. Despite Marriott's own security monitoring systems, the breach went unnoticed for an extended period, and the stolen data was gradually exfiltrated without triggering alarms.

Delayed Discovery Post-Acquisition

The breach was discovered in September 2018, after Marriott had acquired Starwood, but the attackers had been operating undetected for at least four years. The extended duration of the breach emphasizes the sophistication of the cyberattack and the difficulty in detecting advanced persistent threats (APTs).

Data Compromised

Personal Contact Information

The breach exposed a range of sensitive guest information, including names, addresses, phone numbers, and email addresses, which are commonly used for identity theft and phishing attacks.

Massive Global Impact

With up to 500 million individuals affected, the scale of the breach made it one of the largest data compromises in history, affecting both Marriott's reputation and the privacy of millions of individuals globally.

Impact on the Company and Customers

The Marriott data breach significantly impacted both the company’s financial standing and its reputation. Marriott faced intense scrutiny from regulators, consumer groups, and affected customers. The breach led to multiple lawsuits and regulatory fines. Marriott’s failure to detect and prevent the breach earlier raised questions about the effectiveness of its security infrastructure and the due diligence process during its acquisition of Starwood. As a result, Marriott committed to investing heavily in security upgrades, including enhancing its network security and incident response capabilities. For customers, the breach exposed them to a heightened risk of identity theft, prompting the company to offer free credit monitoring services to those affected. The breach also served as a reminder of the critical importance of cybersecurity in the context of mergers and acquisitions, especially when integrating legacy systems into a new organization.

Famous Cyber Attacks / Yahoo Data Breaches

Yahoo Data Breaches

Overview Method of Attack Impact and Consequences

Overview

The Yahoo data breaches were among the largest known breaches in internet history. They occurred between 2013 and 2014 but were only disclosed to the public in 2016. The breaches compromised data from all 3 billion Yahoo accounts. Stolen information included names, email addresses, hashed passwords, and security questions and answers. Yahoo initially underestimated the number of affected users, leading to significant public backlash. Nation-state actors were suspected to be involved in at least one of the breaches. The late disclosure and mishandling of the breach damaged Yahoo’s reputation severely. It also significantly impacted Yahoo’s acquisition by Verizon.

Method of Attack

Attackers used spear-phishing tactics to gain initial access to Yahoo’s systems. Once inside, they forged authentication cookies, allowing them to access accounts without needing passwords. The breaches were persistent, with hackers maintaining long-term access to Yahoo's user database. Although no financial data was reported stolen, the personal information compromised created long-term risks for users. The main threat involved credential theft and account compromise across multiple platforms.

Impact and Consequences

Impact 1

Yahoo faced significant financial consequences when Verizon reduced its acquisition price by $350 million. The final deal valued Yahoo at around $4.48 billion, a sharp decrease from the original offer. This represented one of the largest merger devaluations due to a cyber incident.

Impact 2

The breach destroyed much of Yahoo’s brand trust among users. Many users abandoned their accounts or moved to competing services due to fears over security. This severely reduced Yahoo’s active user base and advertising revenue.

Impact 3

The U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) fined Yahoo's parent company for failing to disclose the breach sooner. This was one of the first instances where a company was penalized for delayed cyber breach disclosure. It set a legal precedent for breach notification regulations in the U.S.

Impact 4

The stolen Yahoo data was sold and distributed on underground forums. This created widespread secondary attacks like phishing, credential stuffing, and identity theft. Many users’ accounts on unrelated services were compromised because of password reuse.

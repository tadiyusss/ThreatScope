The AWS DDoS Attack

Initial Discovery Common Early Exploits and Attacks The Development of Security Patching

Overview

The biggest Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attack ever recorded occurred against Amazon Web Services (AWS) in Q1 2020, with a speed of 2.3 Tbps. The attack broke the previous record set by GitHub's DDoS attack in 2018 and was made public by AWS. This major cyber incident brought to light the increasing strength and danger present-day DDoS attacks. It demonstrated the need for corporations to constantly improve their defenses against changing threats. Although the hack primarily targeted AWS's cloud infrastructure, it affected users globally. It was the biggest attack ever made public, but it wasn't the biggest attack ever (based on other private reports). The event reaffirmed how crucial it is for cybersecurity to embrace a Zero-Trust mindset.

Method of Attack

A CLDAP (Connection-less Lightweight Directory Access Protocol) reflection and amplification attack was used to breach the AWS system. Attackers greatly increased their traffic against AWS servers by using open and unsecured CLDAP servers. Attackers were able to increase the amount of their first attack requests by up to 56–70 times using this technique. Since CLDAP servers answered fake queries, it was challenging to identify the actual attackers. A reflection/amplification DDoS assault that took advantage of UDP flaws was the main threat employed.

Impact and Consequences

Impact 1

To handle the massive assault flow, AWS has to implement extensive DDoS prevention measures.The attack tested AWS's defenses to the breaking point in the first quarter of 2020. Despite the size, AWS was able to keep its customers from experiencing significant service interruptions.

Impact 2

The attack made cloud service providers realize how important it is to have strong DDoS defenses. Businesses using AWS to host their services become more aware of their shared security responsibilities. It highlighted how important it is for businesses to tighten cloud setups.

Impact 3

The event helped the cybersecurity community understand how the DDoS threat scenario is changing. Following the incident, reports and studies have been published to look into the vulnerabilities that were exploited. Since then, cybersecurity techniques have shifted their emphasis to recognizing and preventing reflection and amplification threats.

Impact 4

The importance of implementing a Zero-Trust strategy for network security has been highlighted by AWS and security researchers. The attack demonstrated how even little protocol flaws, such as CLDAP, might be turned into weapons. It encouraged improvements and enhancements in AI-powered, automated DDoS defense systems.

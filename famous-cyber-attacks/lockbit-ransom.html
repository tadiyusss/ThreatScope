LockBit Ransomware

Initial Discovery Common Early Exploits and Attacks The Development of Security Patching

Overview

LockBit ransomware attacks first emerged in 2020 and have rapidly become one of the most dominant ransomware threats globally. The group operates a Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS) model, enabling affiliates to launch attacks using LockBit's tools. LockBit is known for its fast encryption speeds and double extortion tactics, where data is both encrypted and stolen. The group has targeted various sectors, including healthcare, manufacturing, education, and government. LockBit consistently updates its malware, releasing new versions like LockBit 2.0, 3.0, and LockBit Black. Their attacks often include threats to leak data unless ransom demands are met. Despite law enforcement crackdowns, LockBit remains highly active as of 2025. It continues to be a major player in the global ransomware ecosystem.

Method of Attack

LockBit affiliates often use phishing emails, exposed Remote Desktop Protocol (RDP) servers, or stolen credentials to access networks. Once inside, they move laterally and deploy the ransomware payload to encrypt critical systems. The malware uses strong encryption algorithms and can automate parts of the attack to maximize damage quickly. LockBit operators also steal sensitive data before encryption for additional leverage. Their approach combines both ransomware and data exfiltration threats.

Impact and Consequences

Impact 1

Healthcare institutions targeted by LockBit faced life-threatening service interruptions. Hospitals had to cancel surgeries and reroute emergency patients due to locked systems. This demonstrated the devastating impact of ransomware on critical infrastructure.

Impact 2

Businesses attacked by LockBit experienced massive operational downtime and ransom demands reaching millions of dollars. Many organizations faced public relations crises after data leaks. Some companies went bankrupt following unsuccessful recovery efforts.

Impact 3

LockBit's success has encouraged the growth of the RaaS model in the cybercriminal ecosystem. More amateur hackers now have access to sophisticated ransomware tools. This has led to an overall increase in the frequency of ransomware attacks worldwide.

Impact 4

Law enforcement agencies like the FBI and Europol have launched major crackdowns against LockBit affiliates. Some affiliates have been arrested, and LockBit’s infrastructure has been temporarily disrupted. However, the group's decentralized model allows it to recover and rebrand quickly.

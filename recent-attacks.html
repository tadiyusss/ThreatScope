Change Healthcare Ransomware.html

This was a highly disruptive ransomware attack that severely impacted the U.S. healthcare system. Change Healthcare, a major processor of insurance claims, experienced a widespread outage, affecting pharmacies, hospitals, and physician practices nationwide for an extended period. The BlackCat ransomware group claimed responsibility. The financial and operational consequences were substantial, with billions of dollars in claims processing affected and sensitive data of millions of individuals potentially compromised.

LoanDepot Ransomware.html

LoanDepot, a large mortgage lender, suffered a ransomware attack that resulted in the theft of sensitive personal information of approximately 16.6 million customers. The ALPHV/BlackCat ransomware group also claimed responsibility for this attack. The compromised data included names, Social Security numbers, dates of birth, and financial account numbers. LoanDepot incurred significant costs in recovery and remediation.

Kawasaki Motors Europe Cyber Attack.html

Kawasaki Motors Europe (KME) was targeted by the RansomHub ransomware group, which claimed to have exfiltrated 487 gigabytes of data. While Kawasaki initially stated the attack was unsuccessful, the ransomware group leaked the stolen data, including business documents, financial information, and internal communications, after their ransom demands were not met.

Crowdstrike Incident.html

This was not a cyber attack in the traditional sense but a significant IT outage caused by a faulty software update released by the cybersecurity company CrowdStrike for its Falcon Sensor product. The flawed update led to widespread "blue screen of death" errors on Windows computers globally, impacting various sectors, including airlines, hospitals, and financial services, causing considerable disruption and estimated financial damage.

Salt Typhoon Cyber Attack.html

Salt Typhoon is the name given to a sophisticated, likely state-sponsored Advanced Persistent Threat (APT) group with suspected links to Chinese intelligence. This group has been implicated in a series of breaches targeting major U.S. telecommunications providers and government agencies. They have exploited vulnerabilities in network devices and lawful intercept systems to gain deep access and potentially exfiltrate sensitive communications.

Schneider Electric Ransomware Attack.html

Schneider Electric has confirmed it is investigating a cybersecurity incident involving unauthorized access to an isolated internal project tracking platform. Their global incident response team is active, and the company states that its products and services remain unaffected. The Hellcat ransomware group listed Schneider Electric on their leak site, demanding a $150,000 ransom in Monero for over 40 gigabytes of data allegedly exfiltrated from the company's JIRA platform. The stolen data reportedly includes project details, issues, plugins, and user data. JIRA, a project management tool, could contain sensitive information about employees and projects.

ICBC Financial Services Ransomware Attack.html

In late 2023, a ransomware attack by the LockBit group severely disrupted ICBC's U.S. operations, halting U.S. Treasury trades and causing payment delays in the $26 trillion market. Less than a year later, in 2024, ICBC's London branch experienced a data breach. These consecutive breaches at a major global bank serve as a stark warning about the interconnectedness and potential systemic risks within the financial system. Consequently, these events will likely drive increased scrutiny and regulatory pressure on cybersecurity preparedness across the entire financial sector

MGM Resorts Phishing Attack.html

In September 2023, MGM Resorts experienced a crippling cyberattack attributed to the Scattered Spider group, a subgroup of the ALPHV ransomware gang, which disrupted operations across major Las Vegas properties for nearly a week, impacting everything from slot machines to reservations. The subsequent revelation in 2025 of a class-action settlement payout program, addressing data breaches from both 2019 and 2023, underscores the severity of the personal data compromise and the growing industry focus on cybersecurity accountability. The near-simultaneous attack on Caesars Entertainment, who reportedly paid a significant ransom, further highlights the escalating and coordinated cyber threats targeting the lucrative casino and hospitality sector, emphasizing the urgent need for enhanced security measures and a proactive stance against sophisticated ransomware groups.

Being Ransomware Attack.html

In October 2023, aerospace giant Boeing was targeted by the LockBit ransomware group, who demanded a $200 million ransom. Boeing confirmed its status as the unnamed company in a U.S. Department of Justice indictment against Dmitry Yuryevich Khoroshev, the alleged administrator of LockBit, which coincided with international sanctions against him. Despite roughly 43 gigabytes of Boeing's data being posted online after the company apparently refused to pay, Boeing stated the "cyber incident" only affected its parts and distribution business and did not compromise flight safety. This incident underscores the high-value targets ransomware groups are pursuing and the potential for significant data exposure even when ransom demands are not met.

TruePill Data Breach.html

A class action lawsuit alleged that Oracle, a major data broker, secretly surveilled individuals online and offline, creating detailed profiles with sensitive information like location, finances, and health. This data was then allegedly sold to third parties without consumer consent or knowledge. The lawsuit highlighted Oracle's use of "coretag" tracking on numerous websites to intercept communications from individuals with whom they had no direct relationship. A preliminary settlement has been reached requiring Oracle to pay $115 million to affected consumers and implement changes to its business practices. The final approval for this settlement is scheduled for November 14, 2024, with further details available on the settlement website. This case underscores growing concerns about the hidden data collection practices of data brokers and their impact on consumer privacy.
